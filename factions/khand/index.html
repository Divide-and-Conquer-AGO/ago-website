Khand is a land of tribes and chieftains, greatest of these are the Variags. Men who have featured upon the history of the west only twice but fierce men nonetheless. They are a warrior people and hold honour and bravery high. The bravest and greatest warriors are chosen to defend the sons of the Variag Chieftain and ruling noblemen, however only those of Variag blood can join the ranks of the nobles bodyguards. Appearing on the battlefield as 'Variag Nobles', they are swift and deadly. They wield powerful bows and excellent swords and wear the finest armour that the smithies of the tribes can offer. Although this is heavy armour, it is not like the armour of Dol Amroth or the Lonely Mountain as it has been adapted for men of the saddle and affords them speed as well as protection. The pride of the Variags is reflected in these men and their finely honed skills in archery and swordsmanship from atop their mounts are held in high esteem throughout the tribes.

Khand is a large country, stretching from the Ered Lithui down to the deserts of Near Harad and it is predominantly ruled over by the Variags. However there are bands of roaming mercenaries and bandits who terrorize the innocent clans folk of the steppes. Using cunning and a great deal of money, the Variags have managed to persuade these Marauders to focus their fury on the foes of the clans and they have since become powerful allies. They aren't well armoured and they aren't well organized but they are very fast, relatively cheap and perfect for picking off any fool who tries to run from the battlefield. Like vultures they prowl the field out of range of any real threat but ready to strike when a weakness opens up.

The four clans of the Orocarni Dwarves can all field formidable warriors and, united, they make a fearsome force that any foes would do well to fear. They wear strong and capable armour and wield large battle axes whilst wearing shields on their backs to protect them from arrow fire. Hailing from the far off Red Mountains these soldiers, like all Dwarves, fight valiantly in protection of their brothers.

Contrary to popular belief, the dwarves do not always shy away from war and the troubles of others. In truth, if their kin are threatened then the dwarves have been known to rally together in astounding numbers and crush their enemies. The last occurrence of which was the dwarven retaliation to the killing of King Thrór in T.A 2790, whereby the dwarves showed the world that they were a force to be reckoned with in the 'War of the Dwarves and Orcs'. The war called upon all of the seven families of the dwarves, and all answered the call. Now, as then, the dwarves of Erebor are threatened by the vastness of Rhûn and the plague like spread of the shadow of Mordor. They have called for aid from the four clans of the Orocarni and the call has been answered!

As the pastures of the Khan are both vast and ever changing, the traditional methods of border defense do not necessarily apply to the people of Khand. A watchtower or a stone wall is of no use when these nomadic people are likely to wander beyond the boundaries it protects not two weeks after it's construction was completed. Therefore, a wise Khan must employ a more dynamic force, in Khan Arkish's case, the Variag Khuzáta. These captains are responsible for ensuring that if a foreign, hostile force was to make it's way within striking range of any of the Khan's camps, it would be known about well in advance. Given the importance of this task, these captains have promoted to their positions soley because of merit and as such come from a variety of smaller tribes rather than the traditional 'nobility' of Khand. While not the finest warriors or diplomats, these men know the rolling hills and long pastures of Khand like no other.

The Khand of today is vastly changed from the times of Khan Arkish's forefathers. Before, the clans raided and warred with each freely. One would claim dominion before falling swiftly to the next, a cycle only occasionally broken by outside subjugation to an ascendant Haradrim empire. Until recently.

With the return of the Dark Lord, the eye of Mordor has fallen upon Khand for the first time in millenia. The Lord of Men requires armies for his wars, and with Khan Arkish as his chosen vassal the fractured clans of Khand have been brought to heel one by one. But generations of infighting are not easily forgotten, and out of the furthest east there are strange rumors also; of fanatical dissidents, and mountainfolk not seen in generations.

Khan Arkish is no fool, and he has learned much from his master of the art of domination. Inspired by the inquisitors of Durthang, Arkish has organized a new arm of the military under his oldest friend Yiltig. These heavily armored macemen, the Amanta, are well equipped and trained to maintain order in the burgeoning urban environments of the new Khand. Despite their intended purpose they are no less deadly on the battlefield, making them an invaluable tool against all enemies of the Khan within or without.

Khand has no formal military and most men go to war with whatever weapon they have practised with the most. There are exceptions to this rule however and the warriors of Khand are testament to that. When a man has served his time in the ranks of the Khandish Elites but has not lost the taste for battle, they almost always join with the Warriors of Khand. These men have seen countless battles and retain their heavy armour from their past. As the duel swords of the elites are symbolic, the warriors can no longer use them and so, to ensure they still instil fear, they have adopted heavy two handed swords. Performing as the very best dismounted shock troopers that Khand can field, they are proud of their pasts and have little fear of death, making them a truly formidable force on the battlefield. Few men survive long enough to see their service ended in Khand, making these units incredibly rare.

Often, the field of battle is not suited to cavalry and the vast hordes of Khand have to use infantry instead, equipping their warriors with sword, spear and axe. Should a man prove his skill on the field of battle on foot then he may be called into the fighting battalions of the Brotherhood of the Axe, one of three elite groups within Khandish life collectively know as the 'Warlord's Brotherhood'. The Brotherhood of the Axe is the youngest of the four and they wear their heavy armour with pride. Opting to use an axe in honour of the great Khandish hero Hasar; Hasar fought and died in battle against Gondor in T.A 1944 but was said to have fought with the strength of 10 men; a maelstrom in the sea of men, wielding his two-handed axe with fury. In battle they also wield these mighty axes and can carve through the enemy ranks with ease, also, like all Brotherhood warriors they cover their faces so as to inspire fear. Despite their prowess, they are only called into battle if absolutely necessary as their numbers are few. As part of his consolidation of power, Khan Arkish implemented a system of bringing the noble sons of the greatest clans together in his camp. There they would live, ride, and learn of war alongside the Khan's own heir to forge bonds of loyalty amongst the historically combative clans. Thus far they have proven the wisdom of Arkish. They are, to a man, fiercely loyal to Orash and equally skilled in war. In honor of their commander's impressive steed, a gift from Arkish and rumored descendant of the legendary horses of old, they call themselves the White Horse Followers. The title is carried with pride even for those who depart the Followers to succeed their fathers, the new chiefs now inextricably tied to the royal dynasty of Arkish and Orash.

Windriders take their name very deservedly, being the fastest unit on the face of Middle-earth. It is said amongst the tribes of Khand that when the Nazgûl came to the far east to rouse the tribes for war in Sauron's name, the Windriders outpaced them. A feat only accomplished by Glorfindel of Gondolin! The Windriders are a group of warriors who now fight for the Variag's but are descended from the first tribesmen to master horse riding. As such they have spent thousands of years closely guarding their masterful secrets and practising the art of riding. Whilst not the finest warriors of the clans they are still very skilled at fighting atop their horses and they carry fierce sabres, capable of slicing through enemies at top speed. There are none faster than the Windriders and they are a formidable foe.
