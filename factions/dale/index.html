He was a descendant in long line of Girion, Lord of Dale, whose wife and child had escaped down the Running River from the ruin long ago. … Bard took the lead [after the attack on Laketown], and ordered things as he wished, … he had a hard task to govern the people and direct the preparations for their protection and housing. Before Dale was burned by Smaug the Golden, an elite force of archers guarded the city, wielding both bow and spear to defend the Palace of Girion and the king who lived within. After Bard reclaimed the city and slew Smaug, he sought to recreate this force so the generals of Dale would be guarded by capable men; using spears once meant for the Dorwinion King, Bladorthin, Bard equipped his best men. However, despite the abundance of plate armour Bard declined it, instead choosing to garb the archers in chain mail, not wishing to clothe a select few in rich garments while the rest suffered; instead the plate was melted down for tools or sold to fund Dale’s reconstruction.

Regardless of their origin, these men who are garbed in the red of the Girion’s Palace are a force to be feared for they are fine archers that can pick off enemy troops easily, before turning to dispose of any remaining enemy cavalry or infantry with their spears. Any who face Dale in combat would do well to look past the humble garments of these men, for to underestimate them is to risk death.

The Aihwothiuda Horseguards were the elite warriors of shattered Rhovanion, the last of them falling to the Wainriders long centuries ago. Yet with Dale being born anew out of the ashes of dragon fire, the Rhovanion's lost children began to dream. Proud scions of noble lines, long preserved within the Athala Rangers, have stepped out of the shadows at long last, and taken up the name Aihwothiuda once more. Though they were few at first, their numbers swell as they are joined by ambitious merchants, landless sons of Earls, and wealthy mercenaries, all hoping to be named as lords in the reborn realm. Clad in shining mail, riding barded mounts, and armed with lance, bow, and shield, entry into the Horseguard is no small expense, but the potential rewards are tremendous. Named for the fabled past and fighting for a glittering future, the Aihwothiuda Horseguard are the mailed fist of Dale, crashing fearlessly into any who would stand against the reborn Northern realms.

The Athala nobles are the lifeline between the ancient fallen kingdom of Rhovanion and their descendants in the Kingdom of Dale. While the Rangers of Ithilien operate from the west of the Anduin, the rangers of Rhovanion operate through Mirkwood and as far as the Iron Hills. It was from the Rangers that Bard the Bowman emerged to defeat Smaug the dragon, it is also from the ranks of these guardians that the nobles and best warriors of the Kingdom of Dale are recruited. For over 20 generations these Rangers have kept alive the memory of the old ways, wandering Wilderland, carrying messages between man, elf and dwarf, as well as scouting against the dark enemy.

Some nobles in Dale have begun to ape the Southern lords and their disdain for the bow, claiming that only those who meet their foes face to face show true mettle. How soon those fools forget! Dale is a society of hierarchy and status. From the King down to the lowliest peasant, all know their place. All will however fight and in times of need, the Barons will take to the field alongside their mounted superiors; the Earls. Both Earl and Baron wears the same armour and wields the same weaponry but what sets the Earls apart and grants them their elevated status is their owning of a horse. Much like the Earls, the Barons pride themselves on having adapted to the requirements of current threats and not blindly following the ancient traditions of their ancestors. Using their army’s hails of arrows as cover they close in on their foes to meet them with a solid shield wall. By strength of arm and deftness of sword they fearlessly take on any target.

It is said that the men of Rhûn worship dragons as gods, their greatest warriors emulating their ferocity and overwhelming power. Dale has suffered the wrath of a dragon twice before, and they intend never to endure that fate again. To this end, the kings of Dale turned to their allies in Mirkwood and Erebor, and created this corps. Dwarf and elf crafted plated armour, spears, shields, and bows, and trained the best warriors in Dale in their use. To this panoply the men of Dale added their fearsome Black Arrows. Made after the pattern of the arrow that pierced the great wyrm Smaug's breast, these heavy bolts will make a mockery of any armour worn by a lesser creature. Taking the name Hearthguard, these men have sworn that the homes of the Dalefolk will not know dragon fire again. Whether that dragon comes as a great tide of Rhûnic zealots or as a winged wyrm, the Hearthguard will slay the beast and defend their people.

In Dale, ambition burns as brightly in the hearts of the small as the great. While the magnates' and earls' sons dream of titles and lands in a reborn Rhovanion, the poor of Dale and Esgaroth hope for fertile fields and rich pastures south of the Celduin.
