Realm of Lothlórien

Description

“It seemed to him that he had stepped through a high window that looked on a vanished world. A light was upon it for which his language had no name. All that he saw was shapely, but the shapes seemed at once clear cut, as if they had been first conceived and drawn at the uncovering of his eyes, and ancient as if they had endured for ever. He saw no colour but those he knew, gold and white and blue and green, but they were fresh and poignant, as if he had at that moment first perceived them and made for them names new and wonderful. In winter here no heart could mourn for summer or for spring. No blemish or sickness or deformity could be seen in anything that grew upon the earth. On the land of Lórien, there was no stain.” - Power of Lórien

Gûrdhínen "Silent Death": The Elves of Lothlórien have developed a guerilla style of warfare over their long years in Middle-earth and this ensures the safety of their realm as they strike, unseen, from many vantage points. However, among their stealthy ranks is an order of warriors who have mastered the art to a terrifying degree and can strike seemingly from the thin air; they are known only as i Gûrdhínen. They fight for the Lord and Lady of Lórien whenever they call and will often be the silent hand of control on a battlefield. They are a ferocious order who can hide in all manner of terrain, using their Elven cloaks to blend in with any surrounding and then striking with deadly efficiency. On the battlefield they use a unique double-bladed glaive, which they wield with deadly skill, a cloak of the Galadhrim but little armour; this gives them excellent agility and a fine degree of lethality. Whilst they number only few, they can turn the tide of battle in one silent, but mighty, blow. "Berio i dae len..." 'Shadows protect you' - mantra of I Gûrdhínen.

Pronunciation: 'Goor-thee-nen'

Gûrveleg "Great Death": Lothlórien's secret weapon. They are rarely seen on the battlefield and even when they do fight the enemy, they are still rarely seen. Cerin Amroth is a a grassy flower covered hill in Lothlórien of great splendour remarked by Aragorn to be "the heart of Elvendom on earth". On the hill's summit there are two circles of trees in whose branches King Amroth, the previous ruler of Lothlórien, built his residence. King Amroth and his house are long gone but the hill retains its great importance to the Galadhrim. Sentinels of Cerin Amroth are an honour guard who keep watch over this sacred place and ensure that its beauty remains unspoiled. From the topmost point in the inner circle of trees, which was once the highest point in all of Lothlórien, the Sentinels observe the surrounding lands and are even able to keep watch on Dol Guldur itself. Just as the flowers that grow on the hill, Elanor and Niphredil, are coloured gold and white and the outer trees have white bark while the inner circle are the golden Mallorn trees so the shields of the Sentinels bear those two colours as well, symbolizing the things it is their duty to protect. All Sentinels are expected to uphold the legacy of King Amroth, beauty, prosperity, valour and wisdom, and lend their minds or swords, whichever is needed, to the protection or betterment of the Goldenwood. With war looming ever greater on the fringes of Lorien the Sentinels are being deployed further and further from the Hill and now have the name in a merely ceremonial fashion. No longer is the hill of utmost importance, survival has taken precedent.

With the arrival of Galadriel and Celeborn to the Golden Wood came the influence of the Valar and one in particular was revered above all others; The Valian Queen Yavanna. Her domain was that of the flora and fauna of all creation and she loved the beauties of nature above all else and this love was one that passed to the Elves. Wishing to protect the sanctity of their woodland home and to protect all the creations of Yavanna, many Silvan Elves of Lothlórien banded together to create a group of warriors with a raging ferocity and a single goal: to defend the beauties of nature. Marching to battle with fierce two-handed glaives they are more than adept at hacking down the foes of the wood and many an Orc has fallen foul of their wrath.
